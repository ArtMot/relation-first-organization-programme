## Summary judgment

This is a core architecture/integration paper. It is dense and terminology-heavy, but it is largely written as a paper rather than as a development log. It should remain in the canonical foundation stack after Theory of Organization, Charted Organization Theory, and Recursive Chart Learning Theory.

The main public-suitability risks are title opacity, possible overreading of triadicity, and possible overreading of gauge-like language. These risks are addressable through front matter, title repair, and visible non-claims.

## Strengths

- Strong foundation statement: the paper clearly states that triadic mediation is the first closure-complete arity relative to boundary--mediation--visibility requirements.
- Good anti-numerology guardrail: it explicitly says the number three is not sacred.
- Clear non-claims: no Standard Model gauge group, no physical gauge field, no spacetime/gravity/quantum/constant derivation.
- Good adjacent-framework positioning: Peircean triadicity, category theory, Lawvere-style generalized metric spaces, Bregman/information-geometric identities, holonomy/cocycles/sheaf cohomology, and causal/Markov categories are acknowledged without being imported as hidden proof machinery.
- The paper distinguishes relation-modes from objects, which is critical to prevent bad metaphysical readings.

## Main suitability issues

### 1. Title needs public clarification

The current title is acceptable internally but slightly opaque externally.

Recommended title:

```text
Triadic Closure and Recursive Residuals in Relation-First Systems
```

Possible subtitle:

```text
Boundary, Mediation, Visibility, and Closure-Complete Relation Modes
```

### 2. Scope/non-claim block must stay near the front

The current scope section is important and should not be pushed deep into the paper. Public readers must see immediately that the paper is not deriving physical gauge theory, the Standard Model, spacetime, gravity, quantum mechanics, or constants.

### 3. Gauge-like language needs guardrails

The paper mentions gauge-like structure at invariance of closure residuals under admissible chart transitions. This is useful but risky. Before public release, ensure every use of `gauge-like` is explicitly separated from physical gauge-field or gauge-group derivation.

### 4. Reference check required

Because the paper names several adjacent frameworks, the public version should include references or a references-to-stabilize note.

### 5. Notational clarity pass

The role symbols \(\mathsf R,\mathsf O,\mathsf Z\) and the concrete carrier symbol \(R\) are easy to confuse. The current convention section helps; keep it visible.

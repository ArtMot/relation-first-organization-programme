## Summary judgment

Charted Organization Theory is a strong core-foundation companion paper. It reads mostly like a mathematical manuscript rather than a process log. It is suitable for the canonical public corpus after title/front-matter cleanup, metadata insertion, and reference/positioning review.

## Strengths

- Clear foundation statement: chart, observer, information, objectivity, constants, and objecthood are all defined as chart-relative or atlas-relative relation structures.
- Excellent scope discipline: no physical realization claim is made.
- Good terminology section: it clarifies that observer does not mean conscious subject, chart does not mean passive coordinate grid, and information does not mean substance.
- Strong adjacent-framework positioning: FCA, Chu-space, topos/presheaf, sheaf contextuality, and indexed/fibered language are acknowledged without being imported as hidden proof machinery.
- Stable mathematical register in the inspected sections.

## Summary judgment

This file is substantially stronger than earlier sourcebook-style versions. It is mathematically bounded, contains explicit scope limits, and distinguishes inherited FCA/Chu material from programme-specific commitments. It is plausible as a public canonical foundation paper, but the title and front matter need public-facing repair before PDF release.

## Strengths

- The scope is clear: it defines a mathematical foundation only and makes no physical realization claims.
- Prior-art positioning is unusually careful: FCA, many-valued contexts, and Chu-space connections are acknowledged.
- The commitments are explicitly listed rather than smuggled as novelty.
- The paper includes strong self-limitation statements: quotient-face claims, no stronger carrier-free ontology, and metric-free stability limitations.
- The mathematical register is mostly stable and does not read like a chat transcript.

## Main public-suitability issues

### 1. Title is too internal and broad

`Theory of Organization` is programme-native but extremely broad externally. It may invite overreading.

Recommended public title:

```text
Relation-First Organization Theory: Resolution Structures, Proper Organization, and Persistence
```

Alternative:

```text
Relation-First Resolution Structures: Proper Organization, Propagation, and Persistence
```

### 2. Version marker should move out of the title

Use the version in metadata, not the main title.

Bad public form:

```text
Theory of Organization: Relation-First Resolution Structures v0.5.1
```

Better:

```text
Relation-First Organization Theory: Resolution Structures, Proper Organization, and Persistence
Draft version: v0.5.1
```

### 3. Add a plain-language orientation paragraph

The foundation statement is dense but acceptable. Add a short paragraph before or after it:

```text
This paper gives the base mathematical layer of RFOP. It does not attempt to model physics, cognition, or empirical systems. Its purpose is to define how organization is generated from a primitive resolution carrier and how proper organizations, propagation, persistence, and task-relative evaluation are represented inside that structure.
```

### 4. Check theorem numbering and proof register before PDF

The file is long and theorem-heavy. Before public PDF, check that theorem numbers, definitions, and proof labels are stable.

### 5. Confirm citations / references

Because the paper names Formal Concept Analysis and Chu spaces, the public version should include references or at least a references section. If references are missing, add them before preprint/journal use. For a public programme footprint release, this is less urgent but still preferable.

## AI/process leakage

No obvious live-chat or AI-process leakage was seen in the inspected beginning. The document reads as a mathematical paper, not a process log.

## Evolutionary writing

Low risk. The inspected opening is not written as `v0.3 did X, v0.4 did Y`. Good.

## Title audit

The title is the main issue. `Theory of Organization` is meaningful internally but too grand and vague externally. Use a title that names the actual mathematical object.

Recommended:

```text
Relation-First Organization Theory: Resolution Structures, Proper Organization, and Persistence
```

This preserves the programme identity while signalling mathematical content.

## Release decision

Suitable for the first public release after title/front-matter cleanup, metadata insertion, and reference check.

**Decision:** do not rewrite content substantially; repair title/front matter and export after references are checked.

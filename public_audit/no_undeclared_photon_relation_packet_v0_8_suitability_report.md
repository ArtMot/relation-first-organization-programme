## Summary judgment

This is a physics-facing case study, not a foundation paper and not a photon derivation. It should be placed as a NUR/NUB/NUO relation-packet exemplar after the no-go/face papers are in place.

The core framing is good: the paper explicitly says it does not derive the photon and does not deny photons. Its claim is that photon claims require declared relation, boundary, and readout roles.

## Strengths

- Strong non-claims: not a QED derivation, not a denial of photons, not a replacement for gauge theory.
- Good relation-packet structure: boundary/regime, mediation relation, observer/readout.
- Useful distinction among asymptotic photons, detected photons, wave-packet photons, and virtual photons.
- Good derived/imported ledger idea: relation-first derives packet discipline, while QED, empirical constants, and photon facts remain imported or future bridge targets.

## Main suitability issues

1. The abstract contains extensive version-history prose: v0.2, v0.3, v0.4, etc. This must move to changelog/provenance.
2. The title is catchy but may sound like a denial. Add a subtitle: `A Relation-Packet Case Study`.
3. The photon physics claims need reference and scope checks.
4. The equations involving imported photon relations should be clearly labelled imported standard physics, not programme-derived physics.
5. Keep the case-study status highly visible.

## Release decision

Do not export as public PDF yet. Perform public-register repair, especially abstract rewrite and provenance relocation.

## Summary judgment

No Undeclared Boundary is a clean and useful face-corollary paper. It should remain in the bridge/no-go methodology layer after No Undeclared Relation. It is shorter, clearer, and less process-heavy than some surrounding methodology papers.

The main public-suitability issues are title/subtitle clarity and removal of patch-style front matter.

## Strengths

- Clear hierarchy: relation-first primitive -> No Undeclared Relation -> No Undeclared Boundary.
- Good non-claims: boundary is not a separate primitive, not necessarily spatial, not necessarily sharp, and not necessarily physical.
- Clear theorem: Boundary-Face Necessity Theorem.
- Good examples: proper organization, persistence support, approximation, exactness, failure, objecthood, count-volume calibration.
- Useful connection to Theory of Organization, Triadic Closure, Recursive Bridge Calibration, and density/anchoring branch.
- Strong warning that boundary is necessary but not sufficient.

## Main suitability issues

### 1. Title needs subtitle

Recommended title:

```text
No Undeclared Boundary: Scope, Approximation, and Admissibility in Relation-First Inference
```

### 2. Draft subtitle should move to changelog

Current front matter says:

```text
Draft v0.5 — global-boundary clarification, physics examples, and no-go table cleanup
```

Move this to changelog/provenance before PDF release.

### 3. Keep hierarchy visible

The paper should keep the hierarchy near the front because it prevents misreading NUB as an independent master axiom.

### 4. Check examples for overreach

The density/anchoring and GR-facing references are useful, but the public version should make clear they are examples of boundary discipline, not completed physical derivations.
